Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about SuiFlow.

SuiFlow is a groundbreaking platform that allows users to send and receive SUI cryptocurrency using only USSD codes on any mobile phone, regardless of internet connectivity or smartphone ownership. It aims to bank Africa's unbanked population by leveraging the power of the Sui Blockchain.

SuiFlow utilizes Unstructured Supplementary Service Data (USSD) technology, which is a communication protocol used by GSM cellular telephones to communicate with the service provider's computers. This system does not require an internet connection, making it accessible even on basic feature phones (kabambes). When you dial our USSD code, your request is routed through your mobile network to our secure backend, which then interacts with the Sui Blockchain.

"Kabambe" is a colloquial term often used in East Africa to refer to a basic feature phone or an older model mobile phone, typically one that is not a smartphone and often lacks internet connectivity or advanced features. SuiFlow is designed to work seamlessly on these types of phones.

Yes, security is a top priority. SuiFlow leverages the inherent security of the Sui Blockchain for transactions. Additionally, we employ AI-driven fraud protection to detect and prevent suspicious activities in real-time. Your transactions are secured with a PIN, and your assets are protected by the robust architecture of the Sui network.

Transactions on SuiFlow benefit from Sui's ultra-low and predictable gas fees, averaging around ~$0.002 per transaction. This makes sending and receiving SUI extremely cost-effective, especially for micropayments and remittances.

Recipients can instantly withdraw funds to their local bank accounts or mobile money services, depending on the available integrations in their region. More details on specific cash-out options will be available upon launch and in your USSD menu.
